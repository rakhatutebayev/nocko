# SEO Technical Audit: Indexing Coverage (Q1 2026)

## 📌 Executive Summary
Based on the Google Search Console coverage data export from Jan 21, 2026 to Apr 17, 2026, the platform is experiencing a negative trend in Google indexing. Over the past three months:
- **Indexed pages** have decreased from 58 to 27 (a 53% drop).
- **Unindexed pages** have doubled from 43 to 86.

The root cause appears to be primarily related to content quality/crawlability and technical 404 errors, causing Google to drop existing pages from its index and refuse to index newly discovered ones.

## 📉 Trends & Insights
- **Indexed Pages Decrease**: Dropped consistently from ~55 pages in late January down to 27 pages by mid-April.
- **Unindexed Pages Increase**: Climbed from ~45 pages to 86 pages over the same period.
- **Search Impressions**: Generally low (averaging around 5-20/day) with a notable peak in mid-March (Mar 12: 42 impressions), which may correlate with Google's recrawling attempts against failing pages.

## 🛠 Analysis and Recommendations

> [!WARNING]
> **Primary Issue: "Crawled - currently not indexed" (41 Pages)**
> Google visited these pages but chose not to index them. This is the largest factor driving down indexing.
> **Possible Causes**: Thin content, duplicate content, lack of internal linking, or poor page performance/rendering issues. Since you are using Next.js (`next.config.ts`, `.tsx`), ensure your dynamic or CMS-backed pages (like `[slug]/page.tsx`) are rendering critical metadata and content via SSR/SSG.
> **Action**:
> 1. Review the specific URLs for this issue directly in Search Console.
> 2. Ensure your case studies and services pages have rich, unique content and proper internal linking.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Technical Issue: "Not found (404)" and "Soft 404" (34 Pages)**
> Together, these errors account for 34 dropped pages. "Soft 404" means a page is returning a 200 OK status but Google thinks the content resembles a missing/empty page or generic error page.
> **Action**:
> 1. Identify broken incoming links to `nocko.com`.
> 2. Implement **301 redirects** for permanently changed URLs directly in your `apps/website/next.config.ts`.
> 3. For Soft 404s, explicitly ensure your custom 404 logic (or `notFound()` function in Next.js) returns an actual HTTP 404 status.

> [!NOTE]
> **Minor Issues: Discovered & Redirects (11 Pages)**
> "Discovered - currently not indexed" (7 pages) means Google knows the URLs exist (likely from your sitemap) but hasn't crawled them yet, usually due to crawl budget limits. Redirects and Canonicals are typically expected behavior for duplicate layouts, but standard checks should still apply.

## 📅 Next Steps
1. **Log in to Google Search Console** on `nocko.com` to export the specific URLs under the `<Crawled - currently not indexed>` and `<Not found (404)>` reports.
2. **Apply Redirects**: Update your Next.js routing configuration to bridge the broken 404 gaps.
3. **Trigger Validation**: Once changes are pushed and deployed, return to the Search Console to click **"Validate Fix"** since many of these checks are currently listed as "Not Started" or "Failed".
